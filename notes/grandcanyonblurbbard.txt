The Grand Canyon is a steep-sided canyon carved by the Colorado River over millions of years. It is 277 miles (446 km) long, up to 18 miles (29 km) wide and attains a depth of over a mile (6,093 feet or 1,857 meters). The canyon and adjacent rim are contained within Grand Canyon National Park, a United States National Park that is managed by the National Park Service. President Theodore Roosevelt was a major proponent of preservation of the Grand Canyon area, and visited it on numerous occasions to hunt and enjoy the scenery.

The Grand Canyon is one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World and is visited by millions of people each year. It is a popular tourist destination for hiking, camping, rafting, and sightseeing. The canyon is also home to a variety of plants and animals, including the canyon wren, the mule deer, and the Colorado River cutthroat trout.

The Grand Canyon is a place of great beauty and wonder. It is a place where people can come to experience the power of nature and the beauty of the American Southwest.
